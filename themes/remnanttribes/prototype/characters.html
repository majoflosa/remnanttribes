Meet the Characters

  Next Update: TBD

Meet the cast of heroes who find themselves entangled in these fateful events. Learn about their background, allegiances, personalities, and individual contribution to the story.

  •

    of Agenes
    Raised and trained by Kyoji, Beor grows up to be the best fighter in the village of Agenes. Although his combat skills are of renown in his community, questions about the meaning of his distinct appearance and the circumstances that brought him to Agenes have caused many villagers to see him with suspicion and fear. This, coupled with the vague, blurry memory of a childhood tragedy, burdened his teenage years with the challenge of controlling himself in combat – even while training. At 33 years of age, the quest ahead of him will test the mastery of his skills, his temper, and his fitness to be the champion Agenes desperately needs.

  • of Agenes
    A natural born leader, Iva is known in her community for having great courage and poise to tackle big challenges. She was born just one year after the villagers finished settling in the far mountains of The Grasslands, and grew a close relationship with Beor since their young adulthood. Growing up under a close tutelage of Kyoji, her combat skills – especially with the bow and arrow – reached an outstanding level. This makes her as ideal a partner to Beor in his new endeavor against the Empire as she has been in his life.

  • of Agenes
    Having lived most of his life retired from the masses, little is known about the early life of Kyoji. Today, he is revered by everyone among his community for his sobriety, calm, and balance, and consider him their leader despite his contempt for titles. He is also sought for his technical knowledge on a variety of crafts essential for the rudimentary lifestyle of the villagers. As a master of defense arts, one of his most prominent roles has been training the younger ones in combat – Beor and Iva being his closest pupils. Under the constant fear of the Empire’s persecution, Kyoji’s wisdom is largely the reason why any hope still lingers in Agenes at all.

  • of Venthaal
    The blood-thirsty son and heir of the Venthaal Emperor is feared for his impulsive way of leading, and unlike his cold-minded, calculating father, is always eagerly looking for a reason to swing the sword. His experience fighting in the front lines since a young age feeds his confidence to command the Imperial Army – the role he has coveted most. Born into a life of politics and highly structured protocols, his irreverence and preference for thrills and pleasures has created deep doubts among the Imperial Council about his ability to take on his father’s power. The Emperor, whose health is quickly deteriorating, has already granted Liev command of the Army, hoping he’ll take the opportunity to prove himself to the Council.

  • of Venthaal
    A great talent in both military and political strategies earned her enough respect from the Emperor to be named Head of the Imperial Council. She also has great connections and influence among rich lords and families of the Venthaal Empire. Besides respect from her peers, however, such a formidable combination of skills and resources has also earned her suspicion from Liev, who sees her as a threat to his future role as Emperor – a feeling exacerbated by Emperor Walt’s apparent preference for her council over his. As the Emperor’s health declines, Dox’s role becomes more and more prominent in the inner workings of the Empire’s royalty.

  • of Venthaal
    The First Commander of the Venthaal Force is the best known military leader in the Empire and takes orders directly from the Emperor. Stromm’s devotion to his mission of upholding the rules and conventions of the Empire, combined with a natural sense for strategy, battle, and single combat, make him an unquestioned leader among the men he commands. His one and only concern is delivering results to the Emperor, and spends no time worrying about others’ approval of his means, which are usually destructive – much the way Emperor Walt himself is known to work. With tensions growing between Liev – Walt’s heir – and the Council, a big weight in the balance of power depends on who will gain Stromm’s loyalty.

  • of the Feles tribe
    This fierce Feles warrior once showed great promise for his tribe, but a conflict between him and the Feles chieftain turned him into somewhat of a recluse. Though he has become almost a stranger to those around him, his leadership, strength, and ability as a fighter are well known to everyone in the Meadow. Having spent much of his latest years training Drok, who has been his only connection to the tribe and perhaps the closest thing to a friend, he now finds himself yearning for a chance to regain a meaningful role, like he did many years ago.

  • of the Feles tribe
    His seemingly endless energy makes it hard for anyone to be unaware of Drok’s presence in the room, and so has become one of the best known names in the Meadow, though just as a goof by some. He has been tutored in many crafts by Fher, whom he regards as an older brother and aids loyally in any of his tasks – often even without Fher’s invitation. Despite his frequent clumsiness and lack of focus, his energy proves useful in combat when it translates into an unmatched agility and his overly lax personality makes it virtually impossible for him to panic when cornered against an enemy.
