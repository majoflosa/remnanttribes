About this Webcomic

Remnant Tribes is an indie webcomic, based on a fantasy story I started working since 2012, and first launched on this website on April 16th, 2018. The writing, illustration, coloring, editing – and even the web design – is all a one-man project! I have worked on it in my free time after work and weekends, whether it’s laying down new pages, detailing the script, or updating the website.

Throughout these years, I’ve experimented with different art styles and format. Take a look at these images of previous versions:

Yup, it’s come a long way. The story and art have evolved, and continue to do so as I work through each page, except from here on you’re going to be a part of the process. I’ve written more extensively on the early days of the project here.

The Story

This story is an epic fantasy where Agenes (AH’ – juh – neez), a small settlement of villagers, finds itself in the ever-present danger of being caught in the relentless pursuit of the Venthaal Empire. Their hope rests largely on the shoulders of Beor and Iva, the best warriors of Agenes who set off on a journey where leaders and members of different races become involved as they look for ways to stand against the powerful empire. It is a world with rich history of wars, plots, mysteries, and legends – and this age of remnant tribes is about to become a new chapter among them.

About the Author

My name is Mauricio Jose Flores. I studied architecture and graduated in 2012, but immediately veered into web design and have worked as a freelance designer and in WordPress web development since then (my portfolio, in case you’re interested). But long before all that, I’ve enjoyed drawing and storytelling.

I’m a sucker for epic fantasy worlds like Lord of the Rings, Game of Thrones,  Star Wars, and The Legend of Zelda. Comics is my favorite medium because of the way it integrates narrative with drawing – one I can create on my own and, thanks to the Internet, share with the world as a webcomic. Enrique Fernandez, Humberto Ramos, and Sean Gordon Murphy are among my favorite artists.

Other things I enjoy are learning guitar and piano, and watching a good soccer game.

Not That Frequently Asked Questions

When did you start this project?

The idea first started brewing late in 2011, and I started working hard at it in 2012 though it was a very different version back then. For a couple years since, I worked on it sporadically. I started to work on it more seriously around Fall 2016. This blog post tells more about the project’s origin.

What are your influences?

Humberto Ramos has been one of my biggest influences. Joe Mad is also high in that list. As for stories and world-building, I would say The Legend of Zelda takes the cake. Tolkien and George R.R. Martin have sparked a good deal of inspiration, too.

Do you have professional experience in comics?

Nope, none. Zero. Zilch. Nada. This is where all my comic-related work goes, and I’m doing it for nothing more than my own enjoyment, and to keep a motivation to study, practice, and improve my art.

How long have you been drawing?

Since I can remember, though not as consistently as I would like – I’ve taken some long breaks, some short breaks, some periods of intense drawing. During the last year or two, I’ve been drawing around 3 days a week in my free time, usually on something related to this webcomic.

What tools do you use?

Photoshop and an old Wacom tablet for the webcomic. For personal drawings, I occasionally use Colerase pencils and Pentel brush pens.

What’s the update schedule?

You’ll find the date for the next update below each page’s header banner. Not sure if there will be a regular schedule.

Does this story have an ending, or is it ongoing?

Yes, it has an ending. Not anytime soon, but yeah.

Is there violence, nudity, expletives, or anything embarrassing if someone sneaks up on me reading it?

No nudity, no expletives, but there may be an occasional depiction of someone suffering from a serious case of sword-through-chest-itis. I’m trying not to make it too gory, but given the theme and setting of the story, there’s bound to be some heavy scenes.
